go around the world
IN 80 DAYS










Around the World in 80 Days was built in less than 24 hours for HackDFW 2015 by Rice University students Kevin Lin (ELEC/CS '18), Lisa Huang (CS '16), and Abhipray Sahoo (ELEC '16).

We sought to apply the problem of optimizing path lengths to a literally global scale, with the world's most popular travel desinations. Out of the 30 most visited cities in the world, we constructed a complete, weighted, directed graph of air travel routes from any major city. Using Sabre's travel API, we obtained data on the durations, distances, and costs of over 800 flights, represented in our implementation as the edges of our graph.

We start with a user-specified origin, and our algorithm, a modified A* graph traversal, determines the best path through the graph that creates a Hamiltonian cycle originating and terminating at that city. Intermediate nodes represent other cities to visit along the route, and given a fixed number of days per city, the iterinerary takes exactly 80 days to complete.

This project was recognized by Sabre Dev Studio at the event, and our team was awarded a monetary prize for contributing to the developer community.
